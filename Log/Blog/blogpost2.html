Dream big, Dream Kedarnath.

Kedarnath is a small town located in the Rudraprayag district of the Indian state of Uttarakhand. It is situated at an altitude of 3,583 meters above sea level and is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Hindus. The town is famous for its ancient Kedarnath Temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India.
The Kedarnath Temple is believed to have been built by the Pandavas during the Mahabharata era. According to legend, the Pandavas wanted to seek the blessings of Lord Shiva to atone for their sins of killing their own kinsmen in the battle of Kurukshetra. Lord Shiva, however, did not want to forgive them so easily and disguised himself as a bull and fled to Kedarnath. The Pandavas followed him to Kedarnath and eventually found him. They then built the Kedarnath Temple at the same spot to worship Lord Shiva.
The Kedarnath Temple is built in a typical North Indian style of architecture and has a huge Shiva Linga inside the sanctum. The temple is surrounded by the breathtaking Himalayan mountains and is accessible only by foot or on horseback. The trek to the temple is considered to be one of the most difficult treks in India, but the stunning views and the religious significance of the temple make it all worth it.
Apart from the Kedarnath Temple, the town also has other places of interest such as Gandhi Sarovar, Vasuki Tal, and Gaurikund. Gandhi Sarovar is a pristine lake located at an altitude of 4,000 meters and is named after Mahatma Gandhi. Vasuki Tal is another high-altitude lake that is located at an altitude of 4,135 meters and is surrounded by snow-capped peaks. Gaurikund is a small town located at the confluence of the Mandakini and Ganga rivers and is considered to be the starting point of the trek to Kedarnath.
Kedarnath is also known for its rich biodiversity and is home to a variety of flora and fauna. The town is surrounded by dense forests of deodar and rhododendron trees, and is home to a variety of animals such as musk deer, Himalayan black bear, and snow leopard.
In recent years, Kedarnath has faced a lot of natural calamities such as floods and landslides. The town was severely affected by the flash floods that occurred in 2013, which led to the loss of many lives and significant damage to infrastructure. However, the town has since then been rebuilt and efforts have been made to make it more resilient to such disasters.
In conclusion, Kedarnath is a beautiful town that is steeped in history and mythology. It is an important pilgrimage site for Hindus and attracts thousands of devotees every year. The town's stunning natural beauty and rich biodiversity make it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.
