"Dream big, Dream Kedarnath."

Kedarnath is a small town located in the Rudraprayag district of the Indian state of Uttarakhand. It is situated at an altitude of 3,583 meters above sea level and is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Hindus. The town is famous for its ancient Kedarnath Temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India.

"Badrinath: A Journey to the Abode of Lord Vishnu."

Badrinath is named after the Badrinath temple, which is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, one of the principal deities of Hinduism. According to Hindu mythology, Lord Vishnu meditated in Badrinath for thousands of years, and the temple was built on the site where he sat in meditation. The temple is also one of the 108 Divya Desams, or holy abodes of Lord Vishnu.

"Haridwar - Gateway to Land of Gods."

Haridwar is situated at the point where the Ganges River emerges from the Himalayas and enters the plains. According to Hindu mythology, Haridwar is one of the four places where drops of the elixir of immortality, known as amrita, fell from the churning of the cosmic ocean. The town is also associated with several mythological legends and is mentioned in many ancient Hindu scriptures.
